# **BioInfinity HealthOS: Organizational Chart, Roles, & Budgets**

## **Executive Leadership (The Core Brain)**
**Budget Allocation:** 15% of total capital
**Mission:** Orchestrate the 10 subsidiaries, manage the shared technology backbone, and maintain the unified vision.

*   **CEO (Chief Executive Officer):** Visionary leader integrating software, biotech, and clinical operations.
*   **CAIO (Chief AI Officer):** Oversees the Foundation Models, BioCloud, and the Unified Health Graph.
*   **CSO (Chief Scientific Officer):** Drives the biological, genomic, and therapeutic research strategy.
*   **CMO (Chief Medical Officer):** Ensures clinical validity, safety, and hospital integrations.
*   **CRO (Chief Regulatory Officer):** Manages RegIntel, driving FDA approvals and compliance across all subsidiaries.

---

## **The 10 Subsidiaries (The Modules)**
**Budget Allocation:** 70% of total capital (distributed based on phase/needs)

### **1. VitalSphere (Sensing & Data Ingestion)**
*   *VP of Hardware Integrations:* Manages wearable and sensor partnerships.
*   *VP of Data Engineering:* Ensures clean ingestion of massive multimodal streams.
*   *Budget:* Moderate. Heavily reliant on software engineering and API partnerships.

### **2. MediTwin (The Core Intelligence)**
*   *VP of Simulation:* Builds the digital twin architecture.
*   *Head of Multi-omics:* Integrates genomic, proteomic, and metabolic data into the twin.
*   *Budget:* High. Requires massive compute (BioCloud) and top-tier AI talent.

### **3. OncoMind Systems (Cancer)** & **4. NeuroBloom AI (Cognition)**
*   *Heads of Oncology / Neuroscience:* Leading domain-specific research.
*   *Lead Diagnostics AI Engineers:* Building the predictive detection models.
*   *Budget:* High. Heavy reliance on clinical trials and regulatory approval (TrialOS & RegIntel).

### **5. FertiliGen (Reproduction)** & **6. Longevica (Aging)**
*   *VP of Reproductive Health / VP of Longevity:* Guiding the consumer and clinical product roadmaps.
*   *Lead Predictive Modelers:* Forecasting biological trajectories.
*   *Budget:* Moderate. High potential for direct-to-consumer revenue offsets costs.

### **7. PharmaVerse AI (Drug Discovery)**
*   *Head of Computational Biology:* Leads the simulation of biological pathways.
*   *VP of Pharma Partnerships:* Monetizes the discovery engine through B2B deals.
*   *Budget:* Very High. Requires specialized biological foundation models.

### **8. GenomeForge AI (Gene Therapy)**
*   *Head of Genomic Design:* Leads CRISPR and gene-editing computational design.
*   *Budget:* Very High. Deep biotech R&D.

### **9. NanoCure Intelligence (Delivery)**
*   *VP of Nanomedicine:* Develops the programmable delivery mechanisms.
*   *Budget:* Very High. Hard science and physical prototyping required.

### **10. AutoSurg Robotics (Intervention)**
*   *VP of Surgical Robotics:* Leads hardware and software integration for AI-guided surgery.
*   *Budget:* Extremely High. Hard tech, hardware manufacturing, and stringent regulatory hurdles.

---

## **Shared Infrastructure Units (The Backbone)**
**Budget Allocation:** 15% of total capital

*   **Data Platform & BioCloud:** Managed by the CAIO. Ensures secure, federated learning.
*   **RegIntel & TrialOS:** Managed by the CRO. The internal regulatory and clinical trial engine.
*   **TrustLayer (Ethics & Safety Board):** Independent unit ensuring model fairness, lack of bias, and explainability. Managed by a dedicated Chief Trust Officer.
*   **ConsentVault (Security):** Cybersecurity team ensuring patient data ownership and HIPAA/GDPR compliance.
